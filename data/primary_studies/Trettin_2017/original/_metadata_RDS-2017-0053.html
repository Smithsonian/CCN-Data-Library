Description:
Abstract:
Carbon stocks in mangroves in the Zambezi River Delta of Mozambique (East Africa) were inventoried using a stratified random sampling approach from 2012 to 2016. A total 52 plots containing 287 subplots were objectively distributed using a GIS based spatial decision support system (SDSS) to represent the characteristics of mangroves and the operating constraints within the Delta area. The inventory was designed to provide estimates of above- and below-ground carbon stocks for the entire Delta. Data include species, height and diameter at breast height for overstory, understory and dead trees, mass of woody debris, litter, and ground vegetation. Data to estimate soil carbon and nitrogen content to 2 meters depth are also included.
Purpose:
Mangroves are recognized for their numerous ecosystem services and functions that are critical to environmental health and human wellbeing in the regions where they occur and beyond. Although mangroves comprise only 0.7% of the world’s tropical forests, they have been shown to contain globally-significant carbon pools, storing up to five times more carbon than typical upland tropical forests per area. As a result, there is interest in considering mangroves in national climate adaptation and mitigation strategies, yet little research related to climate change and carbon sequestration has been completed in mangroves, especially in Africa. WWF-Mozambique’s project, Sustainable Finance for the Protected Areas System of Mozambique recommended development of a pilot project for carbon sequestration in the mangrove forests of the Zambezi Delta, Mozambique, with aims to conserve mangroves in the Zambezi River Delta through reforestation mechanisms and implementation of sustainable use and management activities with local stakeholders, financed sustainably through carbon markets. The USFS project, The Zambezi River Delta Mangrove Carbon Project: A Pilot Baseline Assessment for REDD+ Reporting and Monitoring is a foundational scientific component of the larger WWF effort, designed to quantify the mangrove carbon stocks to contribute to Mozambique’s REDD+ National Program by building in-country technical capacity and measuring the soil and vegetation carbon pools in the intact mangrove stands within the Zambezi River Delta.
Supplemental_Information:
This project was also used to assess the distribution of mangroves and change in distribution over time. The results of these analyses are available in Shapiro et al. 2015. Lidar data were also collected as part of this project for a portion of the Zambezi River Delta; that data along with remote sensing derived canopy height data are available online (Lagomasino et al. 2016).

Methodology_Description:
An objective-based inventory was used to quantify the above and below-ground carbon stocks within the Zambezi River Delta. The forest canopy height (Lagomasino et al. 2016) was used as the basis for implementing a stratified random sampling design. Inventory plots were randomly assigned using a spatial decision support system (SDSS) and published forest inventory methods. A nested plot design was used for the measurements, whereby subplots were located systematically within the plot. The field sampling protocols were designed to facilitate the calculation of carbon stocks in vegetation components and soils. The approach was to estimate biomass using a combination of direct measurements (e.g, litter and ground vegetation biomass) and indirect measurements of diameter and height which were then used to estimate biomass, or in the case of dead wood, measurements to estimate biomass using published guidelines. The mangrove sediments were sampled in the field, to provide a basis for estimated soil carbon to a depth of 200 centimeters (cm). The field sampling was conducted by a large crew during two missions in 2012 and 2013. Plant biomass samples were processed at Eduardo Mondlane Universityto adjust field weights to an oven dry basis. Sediment samples were returned to the Forest Service laboratory, Cordesville, SC to measure bulk density and determination of carbon and nitrogen content. The forest inventory data were processed using generalized allometric equations, because at the time of the project local or regional equations were not available. Data were analyzed 2014-2016. See the citations below for more details on the sampling, analyses, and data representation.

Source_Contribution:
This data set provides canopy height estimates for mangrove forests in the Zambezi Delta, Mozambique, Africa. The estimates were derived from three separate canopy height models (CHM) using airborne lidar, stereophotogrammetry with WorldView 1 imagery, and Interferometric-Synthetic Aperture Radar (In-SAR) techniques with TanDEM-X imagery. The data cover the period 2011-10-14 to 2014-05-06. Each CHM was resampled to 1 meter x 1 meter to match the spatial sampling of the airborne Lidar data set. All data sets were clipped to the extent of the Lidar coverage and subsequently masked to the regions of mangrove tree cover identified by Giri et al. 2014. Each CHM covered an approximate mangrove area of 6,118 hectares.
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
All data presented are raw data, with the exception of the soil bulk density. The soil bulk density was determined by measuring the volume of the soil core obtained from the specified soil depths and dividing that by the oven-dry (105°Celsius) mass of soil, yield the bulk density in units of: grams per cubic meters.

\Data\Zambezi_Overstory.csv: Comma-delimited ASCII text file containing tree (> 5 centimeters diameter breast height) inventory data collected within the measurement subplots. Variables are defined below.

Plot: Number of the mangrove plot that is a specific area of a mangrove forest selected for mangrove inventory.

Subplot: Number of the subplot that is a small mangrove area within a plot.

Height Class: Tree heights were classified into 5 classes; 1: 2 – 6.9 meters (m), 2: 7 – 9.9 m, 3: 10 – 12.9 m, 4: 13 – 17.9 m, 5: 18 – 29 m.

Species Code: Used to represent mangrove species; 1: Ceriops tagal, 2: Bruguiera gymnorrhiza, 3: Xylocarpus granatum, 4: Sonneratia alba, 5: Avicennia marina, 6: Rhizophora mucronata, 7: Heritiera littoralis, 8: Lumnitzera racemose, 88: Unknown.

Live Diameter: Tree diameter at breast height for live trees, unit: centimeters (cm).

Dead Diameter: Tree diameter at breast height for dead trees, unit: cm.

Dead Code: Used to describe the decay status of standing dead trees; 1: newly died trees, 2: small branches lost, 3: only bole left.

Height: Heights of live and/or dead trees, unit: m.

Live (kg): Fresh weight of vegetation < 1.3 meters (m) in height, unit: kg m-2.

Litter Corrected for % MC: Litter weight corrected for moisture content, unit: kg m-2.

Ash Content (g/g): Proportion of ash.

Litter Corrected for Ash: Litter weight corrected for ash content, unit: kg m-2.

Notes: This was used to note when there were ferns in the ground vegetation, if it is not noted it implies seedlings and other woody vegetation. (Ferns were the unusual occurrence so the plot would have higher values.).
