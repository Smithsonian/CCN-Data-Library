Dataset: Relative sea-level change and driving processes during the past ~4000 years in the Chesapeake Bay, U.S. Atlantic Coast

Authors: Timothy Shaw <tshaw@ntu.edu.sg> and N Cahill and G Barbieri and E  Ashe and N  Khan and M  Brain and M  Mann and J  Clear and R  Corbett and D  Nikitina and J  Walker and J  Pilarczyk and A  Kemp and S  Engelhart and R  Kopp and S  Culver and B  Horton 

Associated publication:
Christie, Margaret A., Christopher E. Bernhardt, Andrew C. Parnell, Timothy A. Shaw, Nicole S. Khan, D. Reide Corbett, Ane Garcia-Artola et al. "Pollen Geochronology from the Atlantic Coast of the United States during the Last 500 Years." Water 13, no. 3 (2021): 362. https://doi.org/10.3390/w13030362

Any use of this dataset must include a citation. The DOI: 10.25573/serc.24526066

The data release contains tidal wetland soil carbon profiles. The data itself is housed in five separate .csv files which can by joined by the core_id and/or site_id attributes. 

shaw_et_al_2023_materials_and_methods.csv - Contains information on materials and methods broken down by study 
shaw_et_al_2023_cores.csv - Contains positional and descriptive information on core locations.
shaw_et_al_2023_depthseries.csv - Contains raw depth-series information for all cores.
shaw_et_al_2023_species.csv - Contains information on the dominant plant species at coring locations.

metadata.xml - Contains a full suite of descriptive metadata with all attribute and variables defined, units clarified, and geographic, temporal, and taxonomic coverages described according to Ecological Metadata Language Standards.

metadata.html - Is a simplified, visual and interactive version of metadata.xml for display purposes.

map.html - Is a map widget showing the geographic coverages described in the metadata. It can be accessed on its own or through metadata.html.

custom.css - Contains display information for metadata.html.

shaw_et_al_2020_associated_publication.bib - Contains bibliographic information for publication associated with this data release
